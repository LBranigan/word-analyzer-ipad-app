Introduction

Word Analyzer ("the App," "we," "us," or "our") is a reading assessment tool designed for use in K-12 educational settings. We are committed to protecting the privacy of children who use our application.

This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and disclose personal information from children under 13 years of age in compliance with:

  • FERPA - Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act
  • COPPA - Children's Online Privacy Protection Act
  • Washington SUPER Act - Student User Privacy in Education Rights

Information NOT Collected

  • We do NOT collect information for advertising purposes

How We Use Information

We use children's personal information solely for educational purposes:

  1. Assess Reading Proficiency - Analyze pronunciation accuracy and fluency
  2. Track Progress - Store assessment results to monitor improvement over time
  3. Generate Reports - Create PDF and video reports of assessment results
  4. Identify Errors - Detect patterns in reading errors to support instruction
  5. Provide Feedback - Generate personalized AI summaries with voice playback
We do NOT use children's information for:
  • Advertising or marketing
  • Creating behavioral profiles for commercial purposes
  • Selling or renting to third parties
  • Training AI models
  • Any purpose unrelated to educational assessment

Data Security

We implement reasonable security measures to protect children's personal information:

  • Encryption in Transit: All data is transmitted over HTTPS/TLS
  • Encryption at Rest: Firebase data is encrypted at rest
  • Access Control: Data is isolated by user account (school/teacher)
  • Authentication: Secure Firebase Authentication required
  • Automatic Deletion: Media files automatically expire after 24 hours

Parental Rights Under COPPA

Parents have the following rights regarding their child's personal information:

Right to Review

Parents may request to review the personal information we have collected from their child.

Right to Delete

Parents may request that we delete their child's personal information from our records.

Right to Refuse Further Collection

Parents may refuse to allow further collection of their child's personal information. This may result in the child being unable to use the App.

Right to Prevent Disclosure

Parents may prevent disclosure of their child's information to third parties (except as necessary to provide the service).

FERPA Rights

In addition to COPPA rights, parents and eligible students (18+) have rights under FERPA:

  • Right to Inspect: Parents may inspect and review their child's education records.
  • Right to Amend: Parents may request amendment of records they believe are inaccurate.
  • Right to Consent: Schools must obtain consent before disclosing personally identifiable information (with certain exceptions).
  • Right to Complain: Parents may file a complaint with the Department of Education regarding alleged FERPA violations.

School Consent

In educational settings, schools may consent to the collection of student information on behalf of parents under COPPA, provided that:

  1. The data is used solely for educational purposes
  2. The school has received this Privacy Policy and our Direct Notice
  3. The school has agreed to inform parents of the App's use

Schools that consent on behalf of parents remain responsible for ensuring parents are informed of their rights.

Changes to This Privacy Policy
